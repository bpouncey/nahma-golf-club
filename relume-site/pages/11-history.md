### Main Narrative Section (cream background, two-column: 2/3 content + 1/3 sidebar)

**Left column — 3 narrative sections:**

**H2: "Founded in 1922"**
Body copy: In 1922, a small group of residents in the Delta County area established a golf course on the shores of Big Bay de Noc. The second course in Delta County, carved out of the surrounding land at a time when golf was still finding its way into American small-town life. Designed with practical sensibilities: honest holes, manageable distances, and greens that rewarded precision over power. The shoreline location was chosen for its natural beauty and flat terrain. The water has been in view from the course ever since.

**H2: "A Public Club From the Start"**
Body copy: From the beginning, Nahma was a public course. No restricted memberships, no exclusivity. Locals played. Summer visitors from downstate found it and returned. The course became a fixture in the rhythms of Upper Peninsula summer life. Over the decades: years of growth, quieter years, ownership changes, and the constant work of maintaining a course against Michigan weather. Through all of it, the course stayed open and the green fees stayed honest.

**H2: "A Hundred Years and Counting"**
Body copy: In 2022, Nahma Golf Club marked its centennial season. One hundred years of rounds on the same land, looking out at the same bay. The greens have been reworked over the decades, but their character, those round dish-shaped putting surfaces, has been preserved. Today the course operates under the same spirit it always has: public access, honest prices, a genuine welcome for anyone who wants to play.

  - Body copy (3 paragraphs):
    - Para 1: The most distinctive feature at Nahma is the greens. Round and dish-shaped, they reflect Old Tom Morris-era design philosophy — the kind of greens that were standard in the sport's early decades and have since been replaced at most courses.
    - Para 2: The dish shape creates a natural ball-collecting feature: centered approaches settle and stay; mishits roll off the edges. A forgiving design for beginners, a genuine test for better players who find themselves fighting tight pin positions.
    - Para 3: These greens have been maintained over a century and are part of what makes Nahma feel like a different kind of golf experience. Playing them is a connection to how the game was originally intended to be played.

### Then and Now Section (cream background, two equal columns with header)
- H2: "Big Bay de Noc, Then and Now"

Left column — Then:
When the course was founded in 1922, Big Bay de Noc was the lifeblood of the regional economy. Fishing, shipping, and timber defined the shoreline. Golf was leisure for a community that worked hard. The view of the bay from the course was taken for granted by players who lived beside it year-round.

Right column — Now:
The bay remains central to the character of the region, now more for recreation and natural beauty than industry. Nahma Golf Club sits on that same shoreline, offering a round with one of the most underrated views in Michigan golf. Visitors from downstate and neighboring states arrive each summer and ask why no one told them about this place sooner.
