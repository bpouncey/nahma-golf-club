#### Main Article Content

**Opening paragraph (large text / lead style):**
Michigan's Upper Peninsula doesn't get enough credit as a golf destination. The combination of affordable fees, light crowds, and genuinely dramatic natural settings makes it one of the best regions for public golf in the Midwest. These are the courses worth planning around.

**Pullquote (sand background, left forest green border):**
"Golf in the UP is what golf used to be. Honest layouts, no pretension, views that make you stop and look."

**H2: "The Courses"**

Course entries (6 courses total, Nahma featured and highlighted):

**1. Nahma Golf Club — Rapid River, MI** (FEATURED — highlighted card with forest green border)
Established in 1922 on the shore of Big Bay de Noc, Nahma Golf Club is one of the oldest public courses in the Upper Peninsula. The round, dish-shaped greens are a throwback to Old Tom Morris-era design philosophy — nothing else in the region plays like them. Nine holes, par 36, 2,875 yards. Green fees start at $18. Walk-ins welcome, no tee time required. It's the kind of course that makes you wonder why it isn't better known.

**2. Sweetgrass Golf Club — Harris, MI**
Part of the Island Resort and Casino complex, Sweetgrass is a full 18-hole resort course with a more polished feel. A different experience than the public courses in this guide — more structured, more expensive — but worth playing if you're in the western UP.

**3. Greywalls — Marquette, MI**
One of the most visually dramatic courses in the Midwest. Carved through rocky outcroppings above Lake Superior. Challenging and spectacular in equal measure. Marquette is worth a trip on its own; add Greywalls and you have a full golf day.

**4. Marquette Golf Club — Marquette, MI**
A historic 36-hole operation in the heart of Marquette. The Heritage course dates to 1929. Excellent conditions, a classic Midwest layout, and a well-run club. The two courses offer very different experiences on the same property.

**5. Terrace Bluff Golf Course — Gladstone, MI**
A local favorite in the Escanaba area with solid course conditions and a friendly, low-key atmosphere. Good value for the region and a comfortable round for all skill levels. Worth adding to an Escanaba-area trip alongside Nahma.

**6. Thunder Bay Golf Resort — Hillman, MI**
More of a destination resort than a local course, but worth mentioning for UP trip planners heading through the central peninsula. Better suited for a dedicated golf trip than a day outing.

**H2: "Planning Your UP Golf Trip"**

- **Season:** May through September. Late June through August is most reliable for weather. Weekdays are light at most courses, no reservation needed at public tracks.
- **Budget:** $15-$50 per round depending on course. The more remote the course, the cheaper and more interesting.
- **Getting around:** A car is required. The UP is large. Plan for 45-90 minutes between courses on a multi-course day.
- **Where to stay:** Escanaba, Marquette, or Munising are the main hubs. Escanaba puts you closest to Nahma and Terrace Bluff.

---

**H2: "Bottom Line"**

The Upper Peninsula is underrated as a golf destination. Not Traverse City or Pinehurst, and that's the point. These are courses with genuine character, on land with real natural beauty, without resort pricing or resort crowds. Play Nahma for the history and the lake. Play Greywalls for the spectacle. Play the rest to fill out a proper UP golf trip.
