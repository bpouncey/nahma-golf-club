The Course

9 Holes Par 36 2,875 Yards Walk or Ride
The Layout

Nine Holes on the Bay.

Nahma Golf Club sits on the shoreline of Big Bay de Noc, part of Lake Michigan's Green Bay. The course plays 2,875 yards from the tips, par 36. It is a walking course at heart. Flat terrain, open fairways, and a lake breeze that shifts throughout the day.

The greens are round and dish-shaped, the style common to courses built in the early twentieth century. They reward precision over power. If you play here once, you will want to know them better before you come back.

No driving range. No dress code. No tee time required. Show up, pay your green fee, and play.

The Setting

Golf on the Shoreline

The course runs along the shore of Big Bay de Noc, the northern arm of Green Bay on Lake Michigan. The lake is visible throughout the round. No other golf course in the region can claim this address.

The Greens

Historic, Round, Dish-Shaped

Built to the design conventions of 1922, the greens are small, round, and bowl-shaped. They hold a well-struck approach shot and reject a poorly hit one. Each green has its own personality.

How to Play

Walk or Ride. No Reservation.

Walk the course or rent a gas cart. Eighteen holes means replaying the same nine. Walk-ins are welcome anytime. Large groups should call ahead.
