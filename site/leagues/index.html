A Living Golf Community

Nahma Golf Club has run weekly leagues every season since well before anyone working here can remember. Three nights a week, regulars show up, play nine holes, and stick around at the clubhouse after. That is what a golf community looks like.

The leagues run from mid-May through early September, following the course's open season. No handicap system required to join. No prior league experience required. You show up on league night, you are in.

The course is a 9-hole public layout on Big Bay de Noc, established in 1922. It is a place where the same people have played the same holes for decades. The leagues are a big part of why.

Joining Is Simple

How to Join a League

The Wednesday All Scramble is fully open — new players are welcome any week, no commitment needed. Just show up at 6 PM or give us a call first.

The Tuesday Men's League and Thursday Women's League are filling up for the season, but both still have room for alternates and subs. If you want to fill in when a regular can't make it, call (906) 644-2648 and we'll get your name on the list.

1

Call Ahead

Give us a call at (906) 644-2648. For the Wednesday scramble, it is just a heads up — not required. For Tuesday or Thursday, calling first is the best way to get on the alternate list.

2

Show Up on League Night

Arrive by 6 PM on your chosen night. Bring your clubs. That is all you need.

3

Play Nine and Stick Around

Nine holes takes about two hours. The clubhouse is open after with food and a full bar. That is where the real league experience happens.
