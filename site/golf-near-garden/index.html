Golf Near Garden,

Nahma Golf Club is the closest public course to the Garden Peninsula — on the same Big Bay de Noc shoreline, established in 1922.

45°55'N · 086°41'W
From Garden, MI

Across the Bay,
About 40 Miles Out

There is no golf on the Garden Peninsula. The nearest public course is Nahma Golf Club, approximately 38 to 40 miles from Garden via M-183 north and US-2 west, roughly 45 to 55 minutes depending on where you start on the peninsula.

Both places share the same bay. Nahma sits on the north shore of Big Bay de Noc, the same body of water the Garden Peninsula flanks on its western side. If you have spent a day at Fayette Historic State Park or a morning fishing the bay, Nahma Golf Club is the natural next stop heading out.

Nine holes, par 36, 2,875 yards. Public access, walk-ins welcome, no tee time required. Green fees starting at $18. Established in 1922, it is the second oldest golf course in Delta County and one of the few courses in the Upper Peninsula with a genuine lakeside setting.

For Garden Peninsula Visitors

The Same Bay,
a Different Angle

The Garden Peninsula draws people for specific reasons: Fayette Historic State Park, the walleye and smallmouth bass in the bay, the deer hunting in fall, and the kind of quiet that is harder to find each year. It is not a place people end up by accident. If you are there, you sought it out.

There is no golf on the peninsula itself. When people staying near Garden want a round, Nahma Golf Club is the answer — and it has been since 1922. The course sits on the north shore of Big Bay de Noc, the same bay you have been looking across from Fayette or from the water. The setting is the main event: lake views from most of the course, older dish-shaped greens, and the kind of pace that matches the rest of a day on this part of the lake.

The clubhouse is open the same hours as the course. Full bar, sandwiches, pizza. It is a good stop for lunch before a round or a cold beer after the 9th. If you are building a few days around the peninsula and the bay, a round at Nahma fits the same rhythm as the rest of it.
