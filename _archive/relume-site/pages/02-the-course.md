### Course Character Section (centered, sand/cream background)
- Eyebrow: "Course Character"
- H2: "Greens That Tell a Story"
- Body copy (3 paragraphs):
  - Para 1: The defining feature at Nahma is the greens. Round and dish-shaped, they reflect Old Tom Morris-era design philosophy — the kind of greens that were standard in the sport's early decades and have since been replaced by flat, tiered modern putting surfaces at most courses. Not here.
  - Para 2: The dish shape means a centered approach settles in. A short-sided miss rolls off the edge and leaves you chipping. Beginners find them honest and fair. Better players find them a genuine test on tight pin positions. They reward accuracy over power.
  - Para 3: Nine holes takes about two hours walking. Cart rental is available. Golf at the pace it was meant to be played, on a course that has been doing this since 1922.
