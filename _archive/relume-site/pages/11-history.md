Body copy: In 1922, a small group of Delta County residents established a golf course on the shores of Big Bay de Noc. The second course in Delta County, carved from the land at a time when golf was still finding its way into American small-town life. Honest holes, manageable distances, greens that rewarded precision. The water has been in view from the course ever since.

**H2: "A Public Club From the Start"**
Body copy: From the beginning, Nahma was a public course. No memberships, no exclusivity. Locals played. Summer visitors from downstate found it and returned. Over the decades: years of growth, quieter years, and the constant work of maintaining a course against Michigan weather. The course stayed open and the green fees stayed honest.

**H2: "A Hundred Years and Counting"**
Body copy: In 2022, Nahma Golf Club marked its centennial season. One hundred years of rounds on the same land, looking out at the same bay. The greens have been reworked over the decades, but their character has been preserved. Today the course operates under the same spirit it always has: public access, honest prices, a genuine welcome for anyone who wants to play.
